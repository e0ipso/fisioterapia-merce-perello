Pediatrics

Pediatrics

Pediatric physical therapy is the treatment of any disease in childhood, both acute and chronic, which disrupts motor development and, therefore, limits the potential for independent adulthood. Applied pediatric physical therapy is an specialty which requires specific knowledge and practice.

Our goal is to help the child achieve maximum potential of independence through observation, assessment, promoting health and wellness as well as knowing how to apply a wide range of therapies and aids of posture or mobility, provide support to their families, from infancy through adolescence and collaborate with other medical specialists from the field of education and social thus to encourage the participation of children in activities of everyday life at home, school and community.

Sport injuries

Sport injuries

The awareness of a large part of the population about the benefits of physical activity on their health has spread this practice more and more. Medical disciplines tend to prescribe exercise as a basic measure to improve life quality and life expectancy.

Unfortunately exercise also involves problems of their own practice like muscle overload, bone and joint pain, muscle fiber rupture, tendonitis … these are very related to the sport or activity that is practiced. The recovery of these injuries require specificity in the rehabilitation process.

The health care of the athlete results in enhancement of performance, physical activity rehabilitation after injury and a faster return to physical activity.

Neurology

Neurology

Central nervous system lesions and spinal cord injuries, acquired brain injury and multiple sclerosis, … and the peripheral nervous system lesions as Guillem Barré or neuromuscular diseases, are benefited from treatment with physiotherapy which improves motor function and quality of life of people suffering them.

The treatment of CNS injuries and the PNS is done individually. After an initial assessment, goals that meet the specific needs of each patient and their family are set to improve the functionality and quality of life.

Back problems

Back problems

The physiotherapy treatment is one of the most used and most effective back pain solutions, both acute and chronic. The role of the physiotherapists in the treatment of these ailments involves the use of techniques intended to reduce pain and improve the patient's functional capacity and a faster incorporation to daily routine activities.

Besides rehabilitating function, physiotherapy also has a preventive function in order to improve the patient's ability to minimize back problems and prevent them from reappearing through an educational process.
